This talk explains fighting games from a few complementary angles: what makes something a fighting game, how `hitboxes` and `hurtboxes` plus `frame data` define “fair” timing between players, why moves behave like rock-paper-scissors (hit/block/dodge/throw with high/mid/low variants), how combos emerge from framedata and recovery, and how the deeper “non-Nash equilibrium” of character-specific options creates mind-game and strategy (learning the opponent’s pattern, setting up baits, and punishing whiffs). Slides: [Fighting Games Explained](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1vkrs71BbN1R07wVkxkk82aWzF12YXGdPMGYaV04Ki3s/edit?usp=sharing).
